## 1. Line Graph

**Prompt**: The graph shows underground station passenger numbers in a major city. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Paraphrase using one of three strategies:
- Synonyms: *"The chart illustrates the number of passengers at tube stations."*
- Word form change: *"The graph shows the passenger numbers at the underground station."*
- Sentence structure change: *"The passenger numbers at underground stations are presented on the graph."*

**II. Overview** — Summarise the main trends (no specific data):
- General direction: *"Overall, the passenger numbers experience significant fluctuations."*
- Key features: *"There are two prominent peaks — one in the morning and another in the evening — indicating rush-hour periods. The lowest point occurs in the mid-afternoon."*

**III. Detail 1** — First significant grouping:
*"In the morning, starting from 100 passengers at 6:00, the number surges sharply to 400 at 8:00, which represents the first peak. It then declines gradually until 10:00, after which there is a slow and steady increase until 13:00, with numbers staying between 200 and 300."*

**IV. Detail 2** — Second significant grouping:
*"In the afternoon, numbers drop rapidly to the lowest point of 100 at 16:00. Subsequently, they climb steeply to reach the second peak of 380 at 18:00. After that, there is a substantial decrease, and by 22:00, the number of passengers has reduced to around 130, showing a continuous decline in the late evening."*

### Sample Answer (Band 7)
> The chart illustrates the number of passengers at tube stations in a major city.
>
> Overall, the passenger numbers experience significant fluctuations throughout the day. There are two prominent peaks — one in the morning and another in the evening — indicating rush-hour periods, while the lowest point occurs in the mid-afternoon.
>
> In the morning, starting from 100 passengers at 6:00, the number surges sharply to 400 at 8:00, the first peak. It then declines gradually until 10:00, after which there is a slow and steady increase until 13:00, with numbers staying between 200 and 300.
>
> In the afternoon, numbers drop rapidly to the lowest point of 100 at 16:00. Subsequently, they climb steeply to a second peak of 380 at 18:00, before falling substantially. By 22:00, passenger numbers have reduced to around 130, showing a continuous decline in the late evening.

**Key vocabulary**: *fluctuation · prominent · surge sharply · decline gradually · slow and steady increase · drop rapidly · substantial decrease · rush-hour periods*

## 2. Bar Chart

**Prompt**: The chart gives information about a country's main exports in three time periods, including a future projection. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Paraphrase:
- *"The graph offers details about the primary exports of [country] in [years] and anticipated figures for [future year]."*

**II. Overview** — Main trends:
- *"Overall, [Category A] has shown an upward trajectory and is projected to maintain its high level, while [Category B] has experienced a notable decline."*

**III. Detail 1** — Main categories with data:
*"In [Year 1], [Category A] stood at approximately [X], while [Category B] was at [Y]. By [Year 2], both had [risen/fallen] to [values]. The projection for [Year 3] suggests [trend]."*

**IV. Detail 2** — Contrasting category:
*"Contrastingly, [Category C] presents a different scenario. In [Year 1], it was at [X], declining to [Y] by [Year 2], with projections further reducing it to [Z]."*

### Sample Answer (Band 7)
> The graph offers details about a country's primary exports across three time periods, including projections for a future year.
>
> Overall, international tourism has shown a clear upward trajectory and is expected to maintain its high level, while meat products have experienced a contrasting decline.
>
> In the earliest period, international tourism and dairy products each stood at around 8 and 7 respectively. By the middle period, both had climbed to approximately 9.5, indicating strong growth. Projections suggest these values will remain stable in the future period.
>
> Contrastingly, meat products present a different picture. Beginning at around 6 in the earliest period, this figure fell to approximately 5.5 in the middle period, with projections indicating a further decline to 5. Compared to the other two categories, which have either grown or stabilised, meat products have followed a consistently downward trend.

**Key vocabulary**: *trajectory · anticipation · upward movement · corresponding to · present a different scenario · downward trend · on a decline · stability*

## 3. Table

**Prompt**: The table shows the amount of waste production (in millions of tonnes) in six countries over a twenty-year period. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Paraphrase with synonym/structure change.

**II. Overview** — Most notable features (biggest/smallest; general pattern):
- *"In each of the recorded years, Country A produced more waste than all other countries combined. Notably, Country B was the only nation to reduce its output over the period."*

**III. Detail 1** — Majority pattern with data:
*"Between [Year 1] and [Year 2], waste production in [Country A] rose from [X] to [Y] million tonnes. Rising trends were also observed in [Country B] and [Country C], which increased from [X] to [Y] and [X] to [Y] respectively."*

**IV. Detail 2** — Contrasting exceptions:
*"The trends for [Country D] and [Country E] were noticeably different. [Country D] saw production increase more than eightfold, from [X] to [Y]. [Country E], by contrast, cut its output by [Z] million tonnes between [Year 1] and [Year 2]."*

### Sample Answer (Band 7)
> The table indicates the quantity of waste generation, measured in millions of tonnes, in six countries across three time points over a twenty-year period.
>
> Overall, the largest country produced more waste than all other five nations combined in each recorded year. Notably, one country was the only nation to successfully reduce its waste output by the final year.
>
> Between the first and last recorded years, waste production in the largest country rose from 131 to 192 million tonnes. Rising trends were also seen in three other nations: one increased from 28 to 53 million tonnes, while two smaller countries saw their totals rise from 4 to 6.6 and from 2 to 5 million tonnes respectively.
>
> The remaining two countries followed noticeably different trajectories. One saw production increase more than eightfold, from just 0.6 to 5 million tonnes. The other, by contrast, cut its waste output by 12 million tonnes over the final decade — the only country to show an overall reduction.

**Key vocabulary**: *noticeable · manage to · rising trends · more than eightfold · by contrast · cut output · waste generation*

## 4. Pie Chart

**Prompt**: The pie charts show the popularity of social media platforms over a three-year period. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Paraphrase:
- *"The three pie charts illustrate the popularity of [number] social media platforms between [Year 1] and [Year 3]."*

**II. Overview** — Main shifts:
- *"Overall, while [Platform A] witnessed a downward trend in popularity, other platforms became increasingly prevalent, with [Platform B] experiencing the most significant growth. Despite its decline, [Platform A] remained the dominant platform throughout."*

**III. Detail 1** — Top categories with percentage data and comparisons:
*"Regarding the most popular platforms, initially [X]% of users preferred [A], which was nearly triple that of [B] at [Y]%. However, [A]'s figure fell steadily to [Z]% by the final year, contrasting sharply with rises in [B] and [C], which increased by [X]% and [Y]% respectively."*

**IV. Detail 2** — Smaller categories:
*"Turning to the remaining categories, both initially stood at [X]%, followed by a subsequent threefold climb for one platform, reaching [Y]% by the final year, alongside a marginal rise for the other."*

### Sample Answer (Band 7)
> The three pie charts illustrate the popularity of five social media platforms across a three-year period.
>
> Overall, the leading platform witnessed a downward trend in popularity, while all other platforms became increasingly prevalent. Despite becoming less favoured, the leading platform remained dominant throughout the period.
>
> Regarding the top three platforms, the leading one initially attracted 59% of users — nearly triple the second most popular platform at 21%, compared to just 10% for the third. However, its share fell consistently to 38% by the final year, contrasting sharply with moderate rises for the second and third platforms, which increased by 4% and 5% respectively.
>
> Turning to the two smaller categories, both began at 5%. One experienced a remarkable threefold rise to 14% by the final year, suggesting rapid growth in user preference, while the other saw only a marginal increase of 3%, reaching 8%.

**Key vocabulary**: *ubiquitous · prevalent · contrasting sharply · marginal rise · threefold climb · dominant · subsequent*

## 5. Useful Phrases by Chart Type

### All Chart Types — Opening Verbs
*illustrate · depict · present · compare · show · indicate · provide information about · give details about*

### Line Graph / Bar Chart
*surge sharply · climb steadily · rise dramatically · decline gradually · drop rapidly · plunge to · level off · fluctuate between · reach a peak of · hit the lowest point at*

### Table
*account for · stand at · record · indicate · amount to · compare favourably/unfavourably with*

### Pie Chart
*make up · account for · represent · constitute · comprise · equal · the largest/smallest proportion · nearly double/triple*

### Map
*was converted into · was replaced by · was demolished · was constructed · was extended · remained unchanged · to the north/south/east/west of · adjacent to · in the centre*

### Process / Flow Chart
*first · then · next · subsequently · finally · is fed into · is converted to · is produced · passes through · at this stage · the process begins with · the final stage involves*
