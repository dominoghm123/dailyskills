## Rising Trends

**Verbs**: increase · rise · grow · climb · go up · jump to · surge · rocket · soar · escalate

**Nouns**: an increase · a rise · growth · a climb · a surge · a boom

**Adjectives**: increasing · rising · growing · soaring

**Adverbs**:
- Sharply: *sharply · dramatically · significantly · rapidly*
- Moderately: *gradually · steadily · considerably · noticeably*
- Slightly: *slightly · marginally · modestly*

**Sentence patterns**:
- *The number of X rose sharply from [A] to [B] between [year] and [year].*
- *There was a significant increase in X, from [A] to [B].*
- *X nearly doubled from [A] to [B] within [time period].*
- *X saw a dramatic surge in [period], reaching [value].*
- *X soared to a peak of [value] in [year].*
- *X climbed sharply to [value], before levelling off.*

## Falling Trends

**Verbs**: decrease · drop · decline · fall · go down · diminish · dip · shrink · plummet · plunge · slump

**Nouns**: a decrease · a drop · a decline · a fall · a reduction · a slump

**Adjectives**: decreasing · declining · falling · diminishing · shrinking

**Adverbs**:
- Sharply: *sharply · dramatically · significantly · rapidly*
- Moderately: *smoothly · moderately · slowly · mildly*
- Slightly: *slightly · marginally*

**Sentence patterns**:
- *X fell significantly from [A] to [B] over the period.*
- *There was a sharp drop in X, declining from [A] to [B].*
- *X plunged to a low of [value] in [year].*
- *X dropped by [percentage/number] over the period.*
- *X dipped to [value] before recovering.*
- *X shrank considerably from [A] to [B].*

## Fluctuating Trends

**Verbs**: fluctuate · vary · go up and down · rise and fall

**Nouns**: fluctuation · variation · instability

**Adjectives**: fluctuating · variable · unstable

**Adverbs**: erratically · unpredictably · sharply · slightly · marginally

**Sentence patterns**:
- *X fluctuated between [low] and [high] throughout the period.*
- *X showed considerable fluctuations, ranging from [A] to [B].*
- *X experienced frequent variations, peaking at [value] before dropping to [value].*
- *X rose and fell erratically over the period, hovering around [value].*
- *X oscillated between [A] and [B], with no clear trend.*

## Stable Trends

**Verbs**: remain stable · stay constant · level off · stabilise · plateau

**Nouns**: stability · a plateau · a constant level

**Adjectives**: stable · steady · unchanged · constant

**Adverbs**: steadily · consistently · relatively

**Sentence patterns**:
- *X remained stable at [value] throughout the period.*
- *X stayed constant at [value], showing no significant change.*
- *X levelled off after [year], stabilising at around [value].*
- *X reached a plateau at [value] and showed little variation thereafter.*
- *X held steady at [value] despite [context].*

## Comparison Language

**Superlatives**:
- *the highest · the lowest · the most significant · the least · the smallest · the largest*

**Comparative adjectives**:
- *higher/lower than · greater/smaller than · similar to · double the level of*

**Comparative adverbs**:
- *considerably more/less · marginally higher/lower · significantly greater/smaller · roughly twice as much as*

**Comparative sentence patterns**:
- *X was significantly higher than Y throughout the period.*
- *Y outnumbered X by [number/percentage].*
- *X was roughly six times more than Y at its peak.*
- *X surpassed Y for the first time in [year].*
- *By [year], X had overtaken Y to become the highest.*
- *X accounted for the largest proportion at [percentage]%.*

## Contrasting / Opposite Trends

- *The opposite trend can be seen when we look at X.*
- *However, there was a different trend for X.*
- *X was the only figure which continued to increase/decrease.*
- *The trend for X was noticeably different from those described above.*
- *While X rose, Y showed a contrasting decline.*
- *In contrast to X, Y remained relatively stable.*

## Sentence Patterns by Trend

### Noun phrase + verb
*The number of students enrolled rose sharply to 500 in 2020.*

### Subject + there was/were
*There was a significant increase in X from [A] to [B] between [year] and [year].*

### Noun phrase + saw/witnessed/experienced
*The city saw a dramatic rise in population between 1990 and 2000.*
*The region witnessed a substantial decline in agricultural output.*
*The market experienced considerable fluctuations throughout the decade.*

### Participle clause (advanced)
*Having peaked at [value] in [year], X then declined steadily to [value].*
*Rising sharply in the first decade, X eventually stabilised at [value].*

## Overview Phrases

The overview paragraph is **critical for Band 6+**. It summarises the most notable features without data specifics.

**Opening the overview**:
- *Overall, it is clear that …*
- *In general, …*
- *At a glance, the most notable feature is …*
- *The most striking trend is …*
- *As an overview, …*

**What to include in an overview**:
- The dominant/most notable trend
- Any significant contrast between categories
- The peak/lowest point if highly distinctive
- Whether the overall direction is upward, downward, or fluctuating

**What NOT to include in an overview**:
- Specific numbers or data points
- Detailed comparisons (these belong in the detail paragraphs)

## Opening / Paraphrasing Phrases

The opening paraphrases the task statement — do NOT copy it verbatim.

**Paraphrasing strategies**:
1. Use synonyms: *shows → illustrates · depicts · presents · compares*
2. Change word form: *consumption (n.) → consumed (v.)*
3. Change sentence structure: rearrange subject/predicate

**Opening templates**:
- *The [chart/graph/table/diagram] illustrates [what it shows] in [place/time].*
- *The [line graph] compares [categories] over a [time period].*
- *The [bar chart] shows the [data] in [number] countries between [years].*
- *The [pie chart] depicts the proportion of [categories] in [year].*
- *The [table] provides data on [topic] across [categories/years].*
- *The [map] shows how [place] changed between [year] and [year].*
- *The [flow chart/diagram] illustrates the process of [topic].*

## Map & Process Language

### For Maps (showing change over time)

**Change verbs**: *was replaced by · was converted into · was demolished · was built · was constructed · was extended · was developed*

**Location**: *to the north/south/east/west of · adjacent to · opposite · on the left/right · in the centre · surrounding*

**Before/After**: *originally · previously · formerly · in the earlier map · by [year]*

**Sentence patterns**:
- *The area to the north of [landmark] was converted into a [new use] by [year].*
- *A new [building/road] was constructed where the [old feature] previously stood.*
- *The [feature] was demolished and replaced by [new feature].*
- *The [area] remained unchanged throughout the period.*

### For Processes / Flow Charts

**Sequencing language**:
- *First/Firstly · Second/Secondly · Then · Next · After that · Subsequently · Finally · At the last stage*

**Passive constructions** (appropriate for processes):
- *The material is [heated/sorted/processed/converted] at this stage.*
- *Once X has been completed, Y is then …*
- *The output is fed back into the system.*

**Process overview pattern**:
- *Overall, the process involves [number] main stages, beginning with [start] and ending with [end].*
