# IELTS Writing Session Quality Checklist

A quality rubric for self-assessing or evaluating the effectiveness of a tutoring session. Adapted from teaching quality standards — useful for reflecting on whether a session delivered genuine learning value.

---

## Session Structure Overview

| Phase | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Warm-up | 2–5 min | Build rapport; understand student context and goals |
| Tutorial | 10 min | Introduce task type, structure, and techniques |
| Guided Practice | 30 min | Write, receive feedback, and revise paragraph by paragraph |
| Score Review | 4–5 min | Give band scores, identify strengths/weaknesses, set next goal |

---

## Phase 1: Warm-Up

**Goal**: Make the student feel comfortable and willing to engage.

✅ Checklist:
- [ ] Engaged in natural, friendly conversation — not robotic or scripted
- [ ] Asked about the student's exam context (target score, exam date, previous experience)
- [ ] Did NOT share screen or use slides during this phase
- [ ] Kept to 2–5 minutes; did not spend excessive time here

**Red flags**:
- Conversation felt unnatural or the student appeared uncomfortable
- Skipped straight to content without any rapport-building
- Ran over time (more than 10 minutes)

---

## Phase 2: Tutorial

**Goal**: Teach the relevant task type structure clearly and efficiently.

✅ Checklist:
- [ ] Correctly identified the task type (Task 1 chart type or Task 2 essay type)
- [ ] Selected a tutorial example of the same type as the student's essay
- [ ] Introduced the 4-paragraph structure (Opening / Overview / Detail 1 / Detail 2 for Task 1; Intro / Body 1 / Body 2 / Conclusion for Task 2)
- [ ] Explained all three paraphrasing strategies with concrete examples
- [ ] Shared screen / materials during this phase
- [ ] Kept tutorial to approximately 10 minutes

**For Task 2 specifically**:
- [ ] Explained the TEE structure (Topic Sentence → Explanation → Example)
- [ ] Demonstrated how to write a strong topic sentence
- [ ] Showed two methods of explanation (Elaboration and Reasoning)
- [ ] Explained how to provide a specific, relevant example

**Red flags**:
- Spent more than 15 minutes on tutorial (inefficient pacing)
- Tutorial examples did not match the student's essay type
- Did not cover all four paragraphs in sufficient detail

---

## Phase 3: Guided Practice

**Goal**: Student writes; tutor gives actionable, high-quality feedback paragraph by paragraph.

✅ Checklist:
- [ ] Student had approximately 40% of the session time for actual writing
- [ ] Gave effective feedback on the student's original essay (if provided in advance): grammar, vocabulary, structure
- [ ] Guided student through writing paragraph by paragraph — did not just correct at the end
- [ ] Feedback was specific and actionable, not vague ("good job" / "this needs work")
- [ ] Suggested improvements in: vocabulary · discourse markers · sentence structure
- [ ] Corrected errors clearly, explaining the underlying rule
- [ ] Maintained smooth pacing — no long unexplained pauses

**Quality signals for feedback**:
- Feedback is **specific**: references the student's actual words/sentences
- Feedback is **educational**: explains *why* a change improves the writing, not just *what* to change
- Student can visibly learn something new from the session — not just receive corrections

**Red flags**:
- Student wrote for less than 30% of the session
- Feedback was generic (e.g., "Try to use more advanced vocabulary" without examples)
- Tutor did not correct errors or glossed over mistakes
- Overall session flow was unclear or disorganised

---

## Phase 4: Score Review

**Goal**: Give an honest, calibrated band score and a clear action plan.

✅ Checklist:
- [ ] Gave an accurate overall band score estimate
- [ ] Broke down the score across all four criteria: TA/TR · CC · LR · GRA
- [ ] Score was realistic — not inflated (a 1-band-gap error is the maximum acceptable margin)
- [ ] Identified the student's **strongest criterion** and acknowledged it
- [ ] Identified the student's **weakest criterion** and explained specifically how to improve
- [ ] Gave at least one concrete example to support feedback on weak areas
- [ ] Structured feedback from **strongest to weakest** — builds confidence before addressing gaps
- [ ] Gave a specific, actionable next step tailored to the student's target score
- [ ] Kept to 4–5 minutes

**Scoring accuracy note**: If the estimated score differs by more than one full band from the student's actual level, the score review has failed its purpose. Honesty matters — inflated scores mislead students and slow progress.

**Red flags**:
- Only gave an overall score without criterion breakdown
- Spent equal time on all criteria (should prioritise the weakest)
- No concrete example to illustrate the main area of weakness
- Did not relate feedback to the student's stated target band score
- Ran over time significantly

---

## Overall Session Quality Markers

A high-quality session leaves the student:
1. **Knowing their current level** — with a calibrated, honest band estimate
2. **Understanding their weakest area** — specifically, not vaguely
3. **Having a concrete next step** — one or two things to practise before the next session
4. **Feeling encouraged** — progress-focused, not just corrected

A low-quality session leaves the student:
- Confused about what to work on
- Deflated from overcritical or unfocused feedback
- With an inflated score that masks real weaknesses
- Having written very little during the "practice" phase
