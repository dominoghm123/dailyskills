## 1. Discussion (Discuss Both Views)

**Prompt**: Some people think that using animals for experimentation is cruel, but others think it is necessary for the development of science. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Paraphrase + state your opinion:
> *"While some individuals consider it inhumane to use animals for experimental purposes, others contend that it is indispensable for scientific advancement. I strongly believe that animal experimentation is indeed necessary for scientific development."*

**II. Body 1** — First view (TEE: Topic → Explain → Example):
- Topic: *"Those opposed to animal experimentation emphasise the moral dilemma..."*
- Explain (Elaboration): *"In the realm of medical technology, it enables the development of innovative diagnostic tools..."*
- Explain (Reasoning): *"Since animals share physiological processes with humans, drugs administered to them help predict human response..."*
- Example: *"For example, the study of animal models has been pivotal in the advancement of prosthetics technology..."*

**III. Body 2** — Second view + reinforce your position:
- Topic: *"In terms of efficiency and cost, animal testing often proves to be a practical investment..."*
- Explain: *"Without it, researchers would need to rely more heavily on human trials, which are not only more expensive but also more ethically complex..."*
- Example: *"For example, during early drug development, researchers can quickly identify harmful compounds using animal models..."*

**IV. Conclusion** — Restate opinion + summarise (no new ideas):
> *"I firmly maintain that animal experimentation is truly essential for scientific development. It propels progress in the pharmaceutical industry and, despite its costs, proves more efficient in terms of time and resources."*

### Sample Answer (Band 7)
> While some individuals consider it inhumane to use animals for experimental purposes, others contend that it is indispensable for scientific advancement. I strongly believe that animal experimentation is necessary for scientific development.
>
> Those opposed to animal testing emphasise the moral dilemma of using sentient beings as test subjects. However, from a medical standpoint, animal experimentation serves as a stepping stone for scientific progress in the pharmaceutical industry. In the realm of medical technology, it enables the development of innovative diagnostic tools and treatment modalities. For example, the study of animal models has been pivotal in advancing prosthetics technology. By observing how animals adapt to artificial limbs, researchers have refined designs that improve quality of life for amputees — benefits that far outweigh the temporary discomfort inflicted on animal subjects.
>
> In terms of efficiency and cost, animal testing often proves to be a practical investment in medical research. Without it, researchers would need to rely more heavily on human trials, which are not only more expensive but also more ethically and logistically complex. For example, during early drug development, animal models allow researchers to quickly identify harmful or ineffective compounds, focusing resources on the most promising candidates and ultimately reducing the time and cost of bringing new treatments to market.
>
> In conclusion, I firmly maintain that animal experimentation is essential for scientific progress. It drives pharmaceutical innovation and, despite its costs, remains a more efficient and ethical alternative to early-stage human trials.

**Key vocabulary**: *inhumane · indispensable · sentient beings · stepping stone · pivotal · refine · diagnostic tools · practical investment · logistically complex*

## 2. Opinion (Agree/Disagree)

**Prompt**: A big salary is much more important than job satisfaction. Do you agree or disagree?

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Paraphrase + clear position with degree word:
> *"Some people argue that a high income is far more crucial than job contentment. I strongly agree that financial compensation is ultimately more important than personal satisfaction at work."*

**II. Body 1** — First reason (TEE):
- Topic: *"First and foremost, a high salary provides the financial stability necessary for a secure and comfortable life."*
- Explain: *"In today's world, where the cost of living continues to rise, a well-paying job allows individuals to meet basic needs and plan for the future..."*
- Example: *"For instance, someone in a demanding field like investment banking may experience high stress, but the substantial income enables them to afford housing and support their family..."*

**III. Body 2** — Second reason (TEE) OR concession + rebuttal:
- Topic: *"Moreover, a substantial income plays a significant role in shaping social status and public recognition."*
- Explain: *"In many societies, earning power influences how individuals are perceived and respected. High-paying careers often confer greater prestige, even when they involve pressure or long hours..."*
- Example: *"For example, professionals such as corporate executives or surgeons may not find every task enjoyable, but their high salaries bring social respect and professional authority..."*

**IV. Conclusion** — Restate position + brief summary:
> *"In conclusion, I firmly believe that a high salary is more important than job satisfaction, as financial security underpins both personal well-being and social standing."*

### Concession Structure (Band 7+ technique — use in Body 2)
> *"Admittedly, job satisfaction contributes to mental well-being and long-term motivation. To some extent, this argument seems reasonable, as a fulfilling role can sustain productivity and reduce burnout. However, without financial security, even the most rewarding job becomes difficult to sustain. Therefore, this does not undermine my overall position that salary must take priority."*

### Sample Answer (Band 7)
> Some people argue that a high income is far more crucial than job contentment. I strongly agree that financial compensation is ultimately more important than personal satisfaction at work.
>
> First and foremost, a high salary provides the financial stability necessary for a secure and comfortable life. In today's world, where the cost of living continues to rise, many people face constant pressure to cover essentials such as housing, education, and healthcare. A well-paying job enables individuals not only to meet these basic needs but also to plan for the future through savings and investments. For instance, someone working in a high-pressure field like investment banking may experience considerable stress, but the substantial income allows them to afford a home, support their family, and enjoy financial independence.
>
> Moreover, a substantial income plays a significant role in shaping social status and public recognition. In many societies, earning power influences how individuals are perceived and respected, and high-paying careers often carry greater prestige, even when they involve long hours and significant pressure. For example, professionals such as corporate executives or surgeons may not find every task enjoyable, but their salaries bring social authority and professional standing that job satisfaction alone cannot provide.
>
> In conclusion, I firmly believe that a high salary takes precedence over job satisfaction. Financial security underpins both personal well-being and social recognition, making it the more fundamental of the two considerations.

**Key vocabulary**: *contentment · financial stability · underpins · prestige · confer · earning power · well-being · social standing*

## 3. Problem & Solution

**Prompt**: In many cities, traffic congestion is getting worse. What are the main causes of this problem? What measures can be taken to solve it?

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Acknowledge the issue + outline what the essay covers:
> *"Traffic congestion has become an increasingly serious problem in cities worldwide. This essay will examine the primary reasons behind this trend and propose effective measures to address it."*

**II. Body 1** — Causes (TEE):
- Topic: *"One of the main causes of urban congestion is the rapid increase in private car ownership."*
- Explain: *"As incomes rise and vehicles become more affordable, more individuals choose to drive rather than rely on public transport. This results in a disproportionate number of cars on road networks that have not expanded at the same pace."*
- Example: *"For example, in many Asian cities, car ownership rates have doubled over the past decade, far outpacing infrastructure development."*

**III. Body 2** — Solutions (TEE):
- Topic: *"One effective solution is the expansion and improvement of public transportation systems."*
- Explain: *"By making buses, trains and metro systems more frequent, affordable and reliable, authorities can incentivise commuters to leave their cars at home. Congestion pricing schemes can further discourage unnecessary private vehicle use during peak hours."*
- Example: *"For example, cities such as Singapore have implemented electronic road pricing, which has significantly reduced peak-hour traffic volumes."*

**IV. Conclusion** — Summarise causes and solutions:
> *"In conclusion, traffic congestion is primarily driven by growing car ownership and inadequate infrastructure. However, expanding public transport and implementing smart pricing policies offer viable pathways to reducing gridlock in urban centres."*

**Key vocabulary**: *congestion · disproportionate · infrastructure · incentivise · commuter · gridlock · congestion pricing · viable pathway*

## 4. Advantages & Disadvantages

**Prompt**: Some companies now allow employees to work from home. What are the advantages and disadvantages of this trend?

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Acknowledge both sides; state position if "outweigh" type:
> *"The practice of allowing employees to work remotely has become increasingly common in recent years. While this arrangement offers certain benefits, it also presents notable drawbacks."*
> *(For outweigh type)*: *"While remote work does present some challenges, I believe its advantages ultimately outweigh its disadvantages."*

**II. Body 1** — Advantages (TEE):
- Topic: *"On one hand, working from home offers significant benefits in terms of flexibility and productivity."*
- Explain: *"Employees who work remotely often enjoy reduced commute times and greater control over their working environment, which can lead to improved focus and job satisfaction."*
- Example: *"For example, studies have found that remote workers report higher productivity levels compared to their office-based counterparts, partly due to fewer workplace distractions."*

**III. Body 2** — Disadvantages (TEE):
- Topic: *"On the other hand, remote work can create challenges for collaboration and employee well-being."*
- Explain: *"Working in isolation may hinder spontaneous communication and teamwork, while the blurring of boundaries between professional and personal life can contribute to burnout."*
- Example: *"For instance, employees who work from home often report feeling socially disconnected from colleagues, which can negatively affect motivation and mental health over time."*

**IV. Conclusion** — Summarise both sides; restate opinion if provided:
> *"In conclusion, while working from home enhances flexibility and productivity, it can also undermine collaboration and personal well-being. Organisations should aim to strike a balance that captures the benefits while mitigating the drawbacks."*

**Key vocabulary**: *flexibility · autonomy · commute · spontaneous communication · burnout · isolation · well-being · mitigate · strike a balance*

## 5. Two-Part Question

**Prompt**: Why has the use of social media increased so rapidly? What impact has it had on society?

### Paragraph Framework

**I. Opening** — Paraphrase + indicate both questions will be addressed:
> *"Social media has experienced explosive growth over the past decade, fundamentally changing the way people communicate. This essay will explore the key reasons behind this phenomenon and examine its broader impact on society."*

**II. Body 1** — Answer Question 1 (Why):
- Topic: *"Firstly, the widespread availability of smartphones and affordable internet access has made social media platforms easily accessible to a global audience."*
- Explain: *"As devices have become cheaper and internet coverage more widespread, individuals in both developed and developing countries can now connect online with minimal barriers."*
- Example: *"For example, platforms such as Instagram and TikTok have attracted hundreds of millions of users in regions where smartphone adoption has grown rapidly in recent years."*

**III. Body 2** — Answer Question 2 (Impact):
- Topic: *"When it comes to its societal impact, social media has had both unifying and divisive effects."*
- Explain: *"On one hand, it has enabled people to maintain relationships across distances and mobilise communities around shared causes. On the other hand, the spread of misinformation and the promotion of unrealistic standards have raised serious concerns about its effect on mental health."*
- Example: *"For instance, social media campaigns have played a pivotal role in raising awareness for charitable causes, while simultaneously being linked to increased rates of anxiety and depression among younger users."*

**IV. Conclusion** — Brief summary of both answers:
> *"In conclusion, the rapid rise of social media is largely attributable to technological accessibility and cultural shifts in communication. While it has connected communities and amplified important voices, its negative effects on mental health and information integrity cannot be overlooked."*

**Key vocabulary**: *explosive growth · accessible · mobilise · misinformation · pivotal · unrealistic standards · attributable to · amplify*

## 6. Paraphrasing the Prompt — Techniques

Every Task 2 introduction must paraphrase the prompt — never copy it verbatim.

### Three Strategies

**Strategy 1 — Synonyms**
Replace key words with accurate alternatives:
- *"using animals for experimentation"* → *"utilising animals for experimental purposes"*
- *"a big salary"* → *"a high income / substantial compensation"*
- *"cruel"* → *"inhumane / unethical"*
- *"necessary"* → *"indispensable / essential / vital"*

**Strategy 2 — Word Form Change**
Shift between noun, verb, adjective forms:
- *"the development of science"* → *"scientific advancement / scientific development"*
- *"important"* → *"the importance of"*
- *"satisfying"* → *"satisfaction"*

**Strategy 3 — Sentence Structure Change**
Rearrange the subject/predicate or use a different grammatical construction:
- Active → Passive: *"The chart shows X"* → *"X is presented in the chart"*
- Clause → Phrase: *"Some people think that X is important"* → *"The importance of X is widely debated"*

### Opinion Phrasing (with degree words)
- *"I firmly believe that..."*
- *"I strongly agree/disagree that..."*
- *"From my perspective, I fully support the view that..."*
- *"I am convinced that..."*
- *"I am of the opinion that..."*

**Important**: Always include a degree word (*firmly, strongly, fully, totally*) — it signals a clear, confident position, which is rewarded in Task Response.
