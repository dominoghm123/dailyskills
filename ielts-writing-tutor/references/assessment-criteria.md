# IELTS Writing: Key Assessment Criteria Explained

Source: Official IELTS Writing Key Assessment Criteria document (IELTS.org)

---

## Overview

Both AC (Academic) and GT (General Training) Writing consist of Task 1 and Task 2. Task 2 carries **more weight** than Task 1 in the overall Writing module score.

**Task 1 criteria**: Task Achievement (TA), Coherence and Cohesion (CC), Lexical Resource (LR), Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA)

**Task 2 criteria**: Task Response (TR), Coherence and Cohesion (CC), Lexical Resource (LR), Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA)

**Note**: Penalty applies for: (a) plagiarised/wholly copied responses; (b) not written as full, connected text (bullet points, note form, etc. are inappropriate).

---

## TASK 1 — Task Achievement (TA)

Assesses how **fully, appropriately, accurately and relevantly** the response fulfils the requirements, using a minimum of **150 words**.

**Academic Writing Task 1** is an information-transfer task relating narrowly to the factual content of a diagram, graph, table, chart, map, or visual input — not speculative explanations outside the given data.

TA assesses the ability to:
- **Select key features** of the information
- **Provide sufficient detail** to illustrate those features
- **Report information, figures and trends accurately**
- **Compare or contrast** by highlighting identifiable trends, principal changes or differences (rather than mechanical description)
- **Present in an appropriate format**

> The critical test: Does the response summarise and compare the data, or just list numbers? An overview is essential for Band 6+.

---

## TASK 2 — Task Response (TR)

Assesses how candidates **formulate and develop a position** in response to a prompt, using a minimum of **250 words**. Ideas should be supported by evidence and examples from the candidate's experience.

TR assesses:
- How **fully** the candidate responds to the task
- How **adequately main ideas are extended and supported**
- How **relevant** the ideas are to the task
- How clearly the candidate **opens the discourse, establishes their position, and formulates conclusions**
- Whether the **format** is appropriate

> The critical test: Does the response take a clear stance and back it up with developed, relevant arguments — or does it waffle, repeat ideas, or miss parts of the prompt?

## COHERENCE AND COHESION (CC) — Task 1 & 2

Concerned with the **overall organisation and logical development** of the message.

- **Coherence** = linking ideas through logical sequencing
- **Cohesion** = varied and appropriate use of cohesive devices (connectors, conjunctions, pronouns)

CC assesses:
- **Logical organisation** of information/ideas, or logical progression of the argument
- **Appropriate paragraphing** for topic organisation
- **Logical sequencing** of ideas within and across paragraphs
- **Flexible use of reference and substitution** (definite articles, pronouns)
- **Appropriate use of discourse markers** — e.g.:
  - Staging: *First of all | In conclusion*
  - Signalling relationships: *as a result | similarly | in contrast | however*

> Red flags: Overuse of "Furthermore", using connectors incorrectly, ideas jumping between paragraphs without logical flow, no clear topic sentence per paragraph.

---

## LEXICAL RESOURCE (LR) — Task 1 & 2

Refers to the **range, accuracy, and appropriacy** of vocabulary used.

LR assesses:
- **Range of general words** (e.g., use of synonyms to avoid repetition)
- **Adequacy and appropriacy** (topic-specific items, indicators of writer's attitude)
- **Precision** of word choice and expression
- **Control of collocations, idiomatic expressions, and sophisticated phrasing**
- **Density and communicative effect of errors** in spelling
- **Density and communicative effect of errors** in word formation

> Red flags: Repetition of the same words, using overly basic vocabulary when richer options exist, incorrect collocations (e.g., "make a progress" instead of "make progress"), spelling errors that cause ambiguity.

---

## GRAMMATICAL RANGE AND ACCURACY (GRA) — Task 1 & 2

Refers to the **range and accurate use** of grammatical resource at sentence level.

GRA assesses:
- **Range and appropriacy of structures** (simple, compound, complex sentences)
- **Accuracy** of simple, compound, and complex sentences
- **Density and communicative effect** of grammatical errors
- **Accurate and appropriate use of punctuation**

> Red flags: Only using simple subject-verb-object sentences (limited range), mixing tenses incorrectly, subject-verb agreement errors, punctuation run-ons (comma splices), overusing passive without appropriate use.

## Practical Scoring Guide for Each Criterion

### When to award Band 7 vs. Band 6

| Criterion | Band 7 signal | Band 6 signal |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| TA/TR     | Clear, complete response with good selection; overview present | Adequate but some features missing, vague or inaccurate details |
| CC        | Logical throughout; cohesive devices used accurately | Generally logical but some mechanical or faulty cohesion |
| LR        | Uses less common vocabulary with some precision and awareness of collocation | Adequate vocabulary; attempts advanced but inaccurate use |
| GRA       | Variety of complex structures; mostly error-free | Mix of simple/complex; errors exist but rarely block meaning |

### When to award Band 5 vs. Band 6

| Criterion | Band 5 signal | Band 6 signal |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| TA/TR     | Partially addressed, mechanical description, no clear overview | Overview present, key features covered (even if imperfectly) |
| CC        | Lacks progression; repetitive; poor sentence linking | Clear overall progression despite some faulty cohesion |
| LR        | Limited and repetitive; frequent word choice lapses | Adequate range; some inaccuracies but mostly clear meaning |
| GRA       | Complex sentences mostly faulty; grammatical errors frequent | Complex structures attempted; errors exist but rarely impede |
