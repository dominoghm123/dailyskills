# IELTS Writing Band Descriptors (May 2023)

Official band descriptors from IELTS.org. A script must **fully fit the positive features** at a given level. **Bolded text = negative features that LIMIT a rating.**

---

## TASK 1: Band Descriptors

| Band | Task Achievement (TA) | Coherence & Cohesion (CC) | Lexical Resource (LR) | Grammatical Range & Accuracy (GRA) |
|------|-----------------------|---------------------------|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **9** | All requirements fully and appropriately satisfied. Extremely rare lapses in content. | Followed effortlessly. Cohesion rarely attracts attention. Lapses minimal. Paragraphing skilfully managed. | Full flexibility and precise use. Wide range with very natural, sophisticated control. Minor errors extremely rare. | Wide range used with full flexibility and control. Punctuation and grammar used appropriately throughout. Minor errors extremely rare. |
| **8** | Covers all requirements appropriately, relevantly, sufficiently. Key features skilfully selected, clearly presented and highlighted. Occasional omissions/lapses. | Followed with ease. Logically sequenced, cohesion well managed. Occasional lapses. Paragraphing sufficient and appropriate. | Wide resource fluently and flexibly used. Skilful use of uncommon/idiomatic items (occasional inaccuracies in word choice/collocation). Rare errors in spelling/word formation. | Wide range, flexibly and accurately used. Majority of sentences error-free, punctuation well managed. Occasional non-systematic errors but minimal impact. |
| **7** | Covers requirements. Clear overview of main trends/differences/stages. Key features clearly presented and highlighted but could be more fully extended. | Logically organised, clear progression throughout. Range of cohesive devices used appropriately (some under/over-use). | Sufficient range for flexibility and precision. Less common items used with some awareness of style/collocation. Few errors in spelling/word formation. | Variety of complex structures. Frequent error-free sentences. Good control of grammar/punctuation but a few errors (don't impede communication). |
| **6** | Addresses requirements. Overview with appropriately selected information. Key features presented and adequately highlighted (details may be irrelevant/inappropriate/inaccurate). | Arranged coherently, clear overall progression. Cohesive devices effective but cohesion within/between sentences may be **faulty or mechanical**. Reference/substitution may **lack flexibility or clarity**. | Adequate range. Attempts less common vocabulary (**some inaccuracy**). Some errors in spelling/word formation (don't impede communication). | Mix of simple and complex sentence forms (**flexibility limited**). Errors in grammar/punctuation occur but rarely impede communication. |
| **5** | Generally addresses task (**format may be inappropriate**). **Recounting of detail mainly mechanical** with no clear overview. **No data to support description.** Irrelevant/inaccurate material detracts. | Some organisation but **lack of overall progression**. Sentences **not fluently linked**. Limited/overuse of cohesive devices. **Repetitive** due to inadequate referencing. | Limited range, **minimally adequate**. **Frequent lapses in appropriacy** of word choice. Errors in spelling/word formation may **cause some difficulty**. | Only limited range of structures. **Complex sentences tend to be faulty.** Grammatical errors may be **frequent and cause difficulty**. Punctuation may be faulty. |
| **4** | **Attempts task but doesn't cover all key features.** Format may be inappropriate. May **confuse key features with detail**. Parts may be **unclear, irrelevant, repetitive or inaccurate**. | Information evident but **not arranged coherently**. **No clear progression**. Some basic cohesive devices (may be **inaccurate or repetitive**). Inaccurate use of referencing. | Only basic vocabulary (**may be used repetitively or inappropriate**). **Limited control of word formation/spelling**. Errors **may cause strain** for reader. | Very limited range of structures, **rare use of subordinate clauses**. Some accurate structures but **errors predominate**. Punctuation often faulty. |
| **3** | **Fails to address task** (may be completely misunderstood). Limited ideas, **largely irrelevant/repetitive**. | **No logical organisation**. Very limited cohesive devices (don't indicate logical relationship). **Difficulty identifying referencing**. | Very limited range. **Errors predominate** and **may severely impede meaning**. | **Errors in grammar and punctuation predominate** and distort meaning. |
| **2** | Answer **barely relates** to task. | Very little control of organisational features. | Extremely limited range; **no control of word formation/spelling**. | **Cannot use sentence forms** except in memorised phrases. |
| **1** | Completely unrelated to task. | **Fails to communicate any message**. | Only a few isolated words. | No rateable language. |
| **0** | Did not attend / did not attempt / totally memorised response. | | | |

Minor errors extremely rare. | Wide range with full flexibility and control. Punctuation and grammar used appropriately throughout. Minor errors extremely rare. |
| **8** | Appropriately and sufficiently addressed. Clear, well-developed position. Ideas relevant, well extended and supported. Occasional omissions/lapses. | Followed with ease. | **6** | All parts addressed (**some more fully than others**). Relevant position (**conclusions may be unclear or repetitive**). Errors in grammar/punctuation but rarely impede communication. |
| **5** | Main parts **incompletely addressed**. Position expressed but **development not always clear**, may be **no conclusions**. Main ideas **limited and insufficiently developed**. Limited/overuse of cohesive devices. **Paragraphing may be inadequate or missing**. | Limited range, **minimally adequate**. **Frequent lapses in appropriacy**. Errors in spelling/word formation **may cause difficulty**. | Only limited range of structures. **Complex sentences tend to be faulty**. Grammatical errors **may be frequent and cause difficulty**. | **4** | Responds **only minimally** or answer is **tangential**. Position **unclear**. Main ideas **difficult to identify, possibly repetitive, irrelevant or not well supported**. **Large parts may be repetitive**. | Ideas evident but **not arranged coherently**, **no clear progression**. ## Scoring Rules

- **Half bands** (e.g., 6.5): awarded when the writing fits most but not all of a band's positive features.
- **Overall Task 1 band** = mean of TA + CC + LR + GRA, rounded to nearest 0.5.
- **Overall Task 2 band** = mean of TR + CC + LR + GRA, rounded to nearest 0.5.
- In the real IELTS exam, Task 2 is weighted more heavily than Task 1 for the Writing module total.
- A **word count penalty** applies for significantly short scripts (under ~80% of minimum). Note this in feedback.
- **Plagiarised or memorised** responses are penalised heavily (Band 0 possible).
